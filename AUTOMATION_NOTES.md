## Content Rules

- Write in Korean.
- Aim for practical, search-friendly blog posts.
- Use a strong SEO title that includes a primary keyword naturally.
- Make the title curiosity-driven without sounding like clickbait.
- Add a short thumbnail phrase that fits inside an image headline.
- Include 8 to 15 relevant hashtags at the end.
- Use a clear title, intro, 3 to 5 sections, and a short conclusion.
- Avoid medical claims that require a licensed professional.
- Prefer actionable wellness, exercise, routine, recovery, and habit-building topics.
- Prefer a warm, trustworthy, easy-to-read tone that feels useful within the first 3 lines.
- Make the body feel blog-like rather than textbook-like.
- Write as if talking to one reader casually but cleanly.
- Prefer natural Korean blog phrasing such as `요즘`, `막상`, `생각보다`, `은근히`, `한 번쯤`, `저도` style expressions when they fit.
- Use short reaction or empathy sentences between information-heavy parts.

## Draft Format

- Start with `Title: `
- Then `PrimaryKeyword: `
- Then `ThumbnailText: `
- Then `Hashtags: ` on one line
- Leave one blank line
- Then write the body in markdown
- Use this body structure in order:
- `## 도입`
- `## 1.`
- `## 2.`
- `## 3.`
- `## 실천 팁`
- `## 마무리`

## SEO Style

- Prefer long-tail keywords such as beginner exercise routines, home workout habits, healthy breakfast ideas, sleep recovery tips, and posture improvement methods.
- Put the main keyword near the beginning of the title.
- Use readable headings that match likely search intent.
- Keep the tone helpful and trustworthy, not spammy.
- Naturally repeat the primary keyword in the title, first paragraph, one middle heading, and the closing section.
- Add concrete specificity such as time, count, beginner level, mistakes, changes after 1 week, or practical checklists.
- Write the first paragraph so the reader immediately feels "this is my problem."

## Title Rules

- Generate 3 candidate titles internally, then choose the strongest one.
- Favor titles that combine keyword + benefit + curiosity.
- Use patterns like:
- `<키워드>, 왜 중요한지`
- `<키워드>, 쉽게 시작하는 방법`
- `<키워드>, 초보자가 자주 놓치는 것`
- `<키워드>, 2주 실천 후 달라지는 점`
- `<키워드>, 실패하지 않는 루틴`
- Avoid exaggerated fear, fake urgency, and unsupported claims.

## Topic Selection Rules

- Prefer topic ideas grounded in current Naver search interest when available.
- If browsing or external trend lookup is available, check recent Naver DataLab search trends first and pick a keyword that fits `운동` or `건강`.
- Favor keywords with clear everyday intent such as beginner routines, diet habits, posture correction, stretching, sleep recovery, walking habits, fatigue recovery, water intake, and morning exercise.
- If no live trend signal is available, fall back to evergreen high-intent topics in the same categories.
- Avoid topics that are popular but too commercial, brand-heavy, or unrelated to practical health and exercise content.
- Avoid repeating the same primary keyword too often across recent posts.

## Engagement Rules

- Open with a relatable pain point or common failure.
- Include at least one "많이 하는 실수" or "이렇게 하면 더 쉬움" style section.
- End with a practical next action the reader can do today.
- Keep paragraphs short and scannable.
- Prefer specific examples over abstract advice.
- Add small conversational bridges such as `사실 이 부분이 제일 어렵죠.`, `처음에는 이렇게 느껴질 수 있어요.`, `그래서 더 중요한 건` when they help flow.
- Mix explanation with light lived-feel commentary instead of listing facts only.

## Tone Pattern

- Start with a real-life scene or common frustration, not a dictionary definition.
- Use 2 to 4 sentence paragraphs most of the time.
- Let headings stay clear for search, but make body copy feel soft and readable.
- Avoid sounding like a report, lecture, or hospital brochure.
- Sound practical, close, and human without pretending to have fake personal experience.
